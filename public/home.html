Do you watch water levels during the week? You should, it provides clues to what Bass are doing and where they are doing it.Water level outflows do the same, outflow creates current in the lake and current can turn on the bite and position Bass differently.

Do you ever do POST ANALYSIS of a days fishing? Let's say they were biting until 1pm and then the bite shut down. Why? Could it be barometric pressure change? Wind Speed change? Wind Direction? Outflow? BassSavvy provides the data to do a detailed analysis of the conditions during your day on the water.

For tournaments and trails will be listed by request, be it by tournament owner or by a fisherman. Also, if there is a lake you would like to see added, please request it. Both requests can be made via the Contact Form. Thanks for your assistance.

We have had various responses that BassSavvy is really great if you live in North Carolina. This is very true, because NC Fishermen have requested LAKES and TOURNAMENTS and TOURNAMENT TRAILS to be added to BassSavvy. Not everyone knows every tournament in their area, if more fishermen participate, the tournament list becomes better and better. Tournament information is local knowledge and we have no way of gathering this information other than from fishermen.

If you see a RAMP missing, please submit it as well, it helps traveling fishermen on new lakes

One of the main features of BassSavvy is it's TOURNAMENT FILTERING CAPABILITY. Here in NC, we have multiple trails that overlap. Some have a required number of tournaments (qualifiers) like CATT Team Trail in order to qualify for the Final Championship of the trail. The tournament filtering allows you to filter the whole list of tournaments down to only the trails/tournaments you are interested in fishing. Clicking on the Date column header will sort them by date allowing you to see date conflicts easily and plan your season of fishing. This was the main reason for creating BassSavvy .

If you are a tournament director Please submit your tournament to BassSavvy, it has improved participation for many tournaments and trails.

If you know a tournament director, spread the word. Charity Tournaments get free banner listings on the lakes they are planned for for months in advance.
